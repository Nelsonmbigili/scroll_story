Chapter I · The Identity

What is Chips Mayai?

★

Chips Mayai, also known as Zege, is Tanzania’s most iconic street food, essentially a french fry omelet. It is made by frying chips in a pan and binding them together with whisked eggs to create a thick, golden pancake. Usually served with a side of Kachumbari (a fresh tomato and onion salad) and spicy sauce, it is the ultimate comfort food enjoyed at any hour, from a quick midday lunch to a late-night snack.

Chapter III · Street Culture

The Heart of
Streets

★

Found on every Tanzanian street corner, Chips Mayai is the ultimate fast food. Cooked over open flames at simple stalls, it is faster and cheaper, peoples' favourite.

Chapter IV · Customization

Make it Yours

★

The base is non-negotiable, but the sides are up to you. Pair it with mishkaki (grilled beef skewers), add kachumbari (a fresh tomato and onion salad) to cut the richness, and finish with spicy pili-pili chili oil if you're feeling brave. Every extra ingredient adds its own bold flavor.

🍢 Mishkaki Charcoal beef skewers
🥗 Kachumbari Fresh tomato salad
🌶️ Pili-Pili The fire sauce
Chapter V · The Verdict

A National
Treasure

★

Some dishes just fill you up, but Chips Mayai defines Tanzanian culture. From the city to the coast, everyone, rich or poor, enjoys it fresh from a street stall. It needs no fancy table or reservation, just a simple hunger that is always met with something extraordinary.
